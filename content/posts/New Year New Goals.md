## Happy New Year!

Well, not quite yet, as I'm writing this, there are about 4 hours left in 2023. 
However, I assume it will be at least 2024 by the time anyone reads this.

This post will be a reflection on the progress I have made this year, as well as a goal-setting exercise for 2024.

## Reflecting on 2023

Looking back on 2023, it was a pretty good year. 
I'd like to take a moment to reflect on a few milestones.

> __Milestones__
> 1. Completed my first full year of college!
> 2. Landed a research position
> 3. Learned Go (to a reasonable degree of proficiency)
> 4. Rediscovered my love of math
> 5. Started a blog

Completing my first full year of college definitely feels like milestone. 
Even though I technically started college last year, we are on the quarter system, so it was only 1/3 of the school year and 1/4 of calendar year.

I landed a research position this year, which I am super stoked about. 
I don't actually know what I am allowed to discuss publicly at the moment, but trust me when I say it's very cool!

This year I learned Go. I began learning the language over the summer when I decided to build a cross-platform archive utility that I could compile to a binary, rather than having to set up a python environment (which was the original implementation). Go has since become my go-to programming language (no pun intended) for personal projects.

I rediscovered my love of math this year, and I have started teaching myself Lambda Calculus for fun. 
Five or six years ago, when I was in middle school, if you had told me that I would be learning math for fun when I got to college I probably would have laughed at you.
At that time, I saw it as a means to an end in understanding things that I was actually interested in.
Now it's one of my favourite subjects.

I also started a blog this year, which is something that I have wanted to do for a long time. 
It's a great way for me to share things that I have learned, and generally express myself.
I intend to continue posting in 2024.

## Looking Forward to 2024

I usually don't bother with resolutions. 
However, I think that goal setting can be extremely powerful, so I will use the rest of this post to set some goals for 2024.
Posting them here should help me to stay accountable, as I will be making another post around this time next year to reflect once again.

> __Goals__ (in no particular order)
> - [ ] Learn a new programming language
> - [ ] Continue exploring math
> - [ ] Blog more frequently (at least once per month)
> - [ ] Make at least one YouTube video

First of all, I would like to learn a new programming language this year, as I think it is a great way to expose myself to new ideas and ways of thinking about various problems in computer science. (I have also wanted to learn a functional programming language for a while now, so maybe 2024 is finally the year)

I would also like to continue exploring math in my free time, I have almost finished all the math requirements for my degree (I just need to take linear algebra), but my excitement about the subject has not faded in the slightest.

I want to blog more frequently this year, at least once a month (ideally more on average). This seems like an achievable goal.

Finally, I have wanted to start a YouTube channel for a long time now, and I will do my best to make 2024 the year that I actually make a video and post it.

## Parting Thoughts

2023 was a good year, here's to making 2024 even better!

If you happen to see this, feel free to leave a comment, it might help me to feel more accountable for these goals!

Best,

Korben
